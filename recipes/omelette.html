Omelette

Description

An omelette (sometimes omelet in American English, see spelling differences) is a dish made from eggs, fried with butter or oil in a frying pan. It is a common practice for an omelette to include fillings such as chives, vegetables, mushrooms, meat (often ham or bacon), cheese, onions or some combination of the above.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Potatoes
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Onion
  • Eggs
  • Tomatoes
  • Green Onions

Steps

  1. Heat oil over medium-high heat in a large skillet. Add potatoes and season slightly with salt & pepper, stirring occassionally, unitl golden brown and crisp. Add onions; cook until soft and starting to brown.
  2. Whisk eggs in a bowl; season with salt & pepper. Pour eggs into the skillet and stir gently to combine with potatoes and onion. Reduce the heat to low and cook until egg starts to brwon.
  3. Flip the omelette and cook for another 4-5 mintues until egg is done.
  4. Serve warm, garnished with tomato and green onion.
